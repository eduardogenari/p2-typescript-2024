In Martha Diamond’s Art, She Took Manhattan

What Frank Auerbach did for Camden Town, Monet did for Paris and De Chirico did for piazzas all over Italy, Diamond did for New York’s masonry.

“Highway” (1984), oil on linen. Diamond took on a massive white building, reconstructing it section by section in her wobbly, wide strokes, and made us see it anew.

Martha Diamond Trust, via David Kordansky Gallery
